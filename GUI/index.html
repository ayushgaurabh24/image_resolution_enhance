Single Image Super Resolution

Single Image Super-Resolution (SISR) is a computer vision task that involves enhancing the resolution of a single low-resolution image to produce a high-resolution counterpart. This is a critical task in image processing, where the goal is to recover fine details and improve the visual quality of images.
